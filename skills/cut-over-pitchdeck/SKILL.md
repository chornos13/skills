name: cut-over-pitchdeck
description: Use when to make pitchdeck more realistic
---

A strong pitch deck avoids doing too much.

1. Don’t oversell

Never make a claim bigger than your proof.
Replace hype with evidence.

2. Don’t overexplain

Explain enough to create belief, not enough to teach the whole business.
Main deck = story. Appendix = proof.

3. Don’t overcomplicate

If investors struggle to understand it, customers probably will too.
One slide. One idea. One clear point.

4. Don’t overestimate

Big numbers must be believable.
Use bottom-up math, not fantasy TAM.

5. Don’t overgeneralize

One customer story is signal, not proof.
Show patterns, not isolated anecdotes.

6. Don’t overcommit

Ambition is good. Unrealistic promises are not.
Milestones must feel executable.

7. Don’t overcompensate

Do not sound defensive about risk or competition.
Acknowledge risk, then show your plan.

8. Don’t overshare

Founder story only matters if it proves credibility.
Personal story must support founder-market fit.

9. Don’t overvalue

Valuation must connect to traction, market, and proof.
Price the company from evidence, not ego.

10. Don’t overrate

Do not say the product is better.
Show where it is better: speed, cost, accuracy, retention.

11. Don’t overstep

Do not claim the future before winning a wedge.
Start narrow. Earn the big vision.

12. Don’t overextend

Do not chase too many users, markets, or products.
Focus first. Expand later.

Final slide-check

Before sending the deck, ask:

Is this clear?
Is this proven?
Is this necessary?
Is this believable?

If not, cut it.
